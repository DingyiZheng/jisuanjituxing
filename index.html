Part 1: Tracing Rays through Lenses

For part one, I simulated the process that rays pass through lens set. The first step is to test whether the ray intersects a lenselement or not. The lenselement is a part of a sphere, which has a center lies in the location of a sphere center, has a radius which equals the sphere radius, and has a aperture which is the diameter of the lenselement. Thus the test of intersection is between a ray and a sphere, just like what we did in the last assignment, the difference is that for the lenselement, we need to use aperture size for further test.

The second task is to reflect the light ray once it hit a lenselement. One tricky thing for this is the io rate are different between trace forward and backward.

Explane the difference between pinhole camera and lens system: A pinhole camera is a camera with no lens.It has a small hole to let the lights go straightly from outside world to the inside sensor. Pinhole canmera has infinite depth of field and do not has a specific focus, as no matter where the sensor plane has been placed, it would always get a clear image. For pinhole camera, all we can change is the sensor depth and the time of exposure.The lens system camera has more parameters and degree of freedom. Since lights are not coming through a small hole, a sigle point in the world space can shoot a beam of light through the aperture, thus might form a very blur image on the sensor. However, the lens can refract the light to get a focus. Focus of a lens is determined by the refractive rate of the lens. To zoom in and zoom out is to change the distance between a lens and the sensor plane and can not be achieved by a pinhole camera.
